Philosophy

Philosophy is an intelectual discipline that is very hard to characterize. It tackles the most abstract and difficult questions that humanity asked itself, like 'What is reality', 'What is knowledge', 'What is the correct way to act', 'What is the perfect society', or 'What is to be human'.

It was born in ancient Greece, and constitutes the intellectual tradition of the West.

Some of the questions philosophers asked themselves helped shape intellectual fields not much related to philosophy, like computer science or biology.
